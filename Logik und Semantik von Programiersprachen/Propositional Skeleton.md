

A formula in the theory of equality

$$x = y \land ((y =z \land \neg (x=z)) \lor x = z)$$
We replace the atoms by boolean variables:

$$\begin{align}
&p \mapsto x = y\\
&q\mapsto y= z\\
&r\mapsto x = z
\end{align}$$
The propositional skeleton of the above formula is:
$$p \land ((q \land \neg r) \lor r)$$
